Contact Us

Helping travel businesses succeed for over 45 years!

Major Travel PLC has been trading for over 45 years. We are one of the most established tour operators and flight consolidators in the UK. As a company that exclusively sells through the travel trade and not direct to the consumer, we only succeed when our travel agents succeed.

Major Independent Partners is designed for those that want to start their own travel businesses. We provide all the tools, training technology, product and infrastructure you need to run a successful travel businesses. We have a range of options to suit different business types. You can simply start your own travel brand and drive traffic and we handle everything else- from reservations to fulfilment. If you want to be more involved, you can manage everything; including using your own content, contracting with suppliers, reservations and fulfilment. You will just use our licensing, technology and infrastructure to be compliant with UK laws.

ABTA is the UK travel association for tour operators and travel agents, and its aim is to provide protection to the UK’s holidaymakers. It tries to enforce high standards from its members, so booking a holiday going through a provider that’s an ABTA member offers benefits over booking with an agent that is not a member. Many of the holidays sold by ABTA members are protected in the event of the travel company going out of business. ABTA itself protects around 3.7 million holidaymakers every year, and from 2008 to 2013 ABTA paid out £19m to travellers whose holidays were cancelled or in jeopardy.

All bookings that included a flight made by us are protected by ATOL. Even with ATOL protected bookings, your money is held in the trust account offering a secondary layer of protection.

You will be issued with an ATOL certificate immediately upon payment. Our ATOL number is 2933. You can verify the ATOL number here:

http://publicapps.caa.co.uk/modalapplication.aspx?appid=2

IATA is a body that protects the interests of airlines. In the event of our insolvency, IATA protects the airlines financially for any money we might owe them. Under IATA rules, an airline must honour any standard published fare ticket issued by an IATA agent, even if the agent ceases trading. Under Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974, the credit card company is jointly and severally liable for any breach of contract or misrepresentation by the retailer or trader. That’s means that in the event of us becoming insolvent, you would be entitled to claim the entire cost of your holiday from your credit card company. You don’t even have to pay the entire amount of the holiday by credit card. Provided you have paid the deposit by credit card, you are covered for the whole amount you have paid for the holiday.
